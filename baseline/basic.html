Trading

Investing

Trading is buying or selling stocks or bonds based on market presented opportunities with the goal of making short term profits. The trader has no interest in holding the assets long term.
Investing is buying stock shares or bonds and holding them for a period of time, usually longer than 6 months hoping that they go up in value as they have done historically over the long run.Profits can be enhanced through reinvestments of dividends or compounding of returns in assets that increase in value.

Risk or Return

What one should invest into?

Risk is what an investor is willing bet in order to achieve a profit. Assets go up and down in value and when making a trade an investor takes the chance of being wrong and the price moving opposite of the desired direction leading to a short term loss.

Risk is quantified by the standard deviation of returns on an asset also referred as volatility. The larger the standard deviation of returns the more volatile the asset, which means that the asset is likely to generate high positive and high negative returns.
An asset class is a group of securities that are similar in a fundamental way and today there are various asset classes available to investors. The main categories are Equities, Fixed Income, Cash Instruments, Commodities and Real Estate. Each category has different fundamentals driving performance.
